Cities We Operate In

Toronto

In the post-2008 global business environment, Toronto has emerged as the second largest financial centre in North America and ranks as a ‘Top Ten’ global financial centre. The Toronto region is the hub of the Canadian financial industry – banks, investment firms, pension funds and insurance companies.

Vancouver

The Vancouver financial district contains numerous federal and non-governmental organizations (NGO) offices, Overseas missions and consulates, and headquarter and branch offices of national and international banks and financial services, accounting and law firms.

New York City

Anchored on Wall Street in the Financial District, New York City is known as the most financially powerful city and the leading financial center of the world. Home to the New York Stock Exchange and NASDAQ, as well as many of the worlds largest financial institutions.

Chicago

The Loop - Chicago's central business district - is the second largest commercial business district in the United States. Home to the Aon Corporation, Chase Bank, Exelon, United Airlines, Boeing, and Ventas Inc.
